Kayode Olumoyin

I received my Ph.D. in Computational Science from Middle Tennessee State University under Dr. Abdul Khaliq. My research interest is in Data-driven neural networks for differential equations and nonlocal modeling. My codes are on Github at https://github.com/okayode. My work involves learning complex patterns from data, equation learning, parameter identification, adaptive neuro-fuzzy inference system and inverse problems. We have a paper on infectious disease modeling MDPI-COVID, where we propose that mitigation measures including; governmental actions and public responses force the model parameters in an infectious disease model to be time-dependent functions. In our approach, we do not need to explicitly specify these time-dependent functions, instead, we learn them by setting them to be outputs of multilayer perceptrons (MLPs) that are connected to a larger MLP that predicts the solution to the epidemiological model. Currently, we are working on a model that considers the addition of a spatial structure to the model. I am also interested in the discovery of governing equations of reaction-diffusion models such as the FitzHugh-Nagumo equations with nonlocal diffusion. In these models, the data-driven approach can detect a nonlinear diffusion term from sparse experimental data.
