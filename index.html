Kayode Olumoyin

I received my Ph.D. in Computational Science from Middle Tennessee State University under Dr. Abdul Khaliq. My research interest is in Data-driven neural networks for differential equations and nonlocal modeling. My codes are on Github at https://github.com/okayode. We have a paper on infectious disease modeling, where we simulate the impact of mitigation measures on the transmission of COVID-19, we proposed that mitigation measures including; governmental actions and public responses force the model parameters in an infectious disease model to be time-dependent functions. In our approach, we do not need to explicitly specify these time-dependent functions, instead, we learn them by setting them to be outputs of multilayer perceptrons (MLPs) that are connected to a larger MLP that predicts the solution to the epidemiological model, see time-varying transmission model. Mutating variants of COVID-19 were reported across many US states since 2021. We proposed a multi-variant model for the COVID-19 pandemic, where we analyze the heterogeneity in the time-varying transmission rates for each variant in the presence of pharmaceutical and non-pharmaceutical mitigation measures, see multi-variant model. Currently, we are working on a spatial model to analyze the propagation of COVID-19 across the USA. I am also interested in the discovery of governing equations of reaction-diffusion models such as the FitzHugh-Nagumo equations with nonlocal diffusion. In these models, the data-driven approach can detect complex pattern from sparse experimental data.
